Wellcome!

This is a demonstration application showing the functioning of Ferrybot, a prototype of an intelligent conversational agent that works in the context of peer support. In our conception, it can communicate with peer support participants and administrators in instant messaging software, acting as a facilitator to help administrators do their jobs better and as a listener and advisor to address the communication needs of members online at any time. The technology behind it is based on state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs), enabling it to understand specific application scenarios, comprehend user text input and respond naturally.

In the context of peer support, we envisioned five specific scenarios involving Ferrybot. In the demo page for each scenario, you can try to have a dynamic conversation with Ferrybot. These interactions typically require you to take on the role of a participant or supporter in peer support. We'd like you to experience each scenario in turn and give us your feedback on the concept. Further, if you are interested or can even provide us with opportunities for collaboration, that's what we want!

Note that this is just a rough and basic prototype of a chatbot based on LLMs, the core of which is to convey the concept of Ferrybot in the peer support scenario. It is not perfect, and we cannot guarantee that it will deliver all answers appropriately. To respect your privacy, we declare that we will not record any of your actions and inputs during interactions. Thank you for your understanding!

In this scenario, Ferrybot acts as a caring intermediary, reaching out to members of the peer support group who have been less active recently. The aim is to proactively and privately check on their overall mental and physical wellbeing, including aspects such as mood, sleep, diet, and exercise. This proactive approach helps ensure that all members feel supported and engaged, even when they are not actively participating in group activities.

For new members of the Glasgow-based peer support organization, Ferrybot serves as a guide to help them find the most appropriate subgroup that matches their specific needs. By gathering information about the members' age, gender, interests, and preferred locations, Ferrybot can accurately recommend the best-fit group.

This scenario involves Ferrybot organizing and coordinating group activities by collecting and analysing members' availability and preferences. Ferrybot facilitates discussions within the group to determine the best time and type of activity that suits the majority. This coordination helps in planning events that are enjoyable and accessible to all, enhancing the group's cohesion and social interaction.
